Mechanical engineering is the branch of engineering focused on designing, analyzing, manufacturing, and maintaining mechanical systems using principles of physics, mathematics, and materials science.
Overview
Mechanical engineering is one of the oldest and broadest engineering disciplines, often called the mother branch of engineering due to its foundational role and extensive applications in technology and industry. It involves the study of mechanics, dynamics, thermodynamics, materials science, structural analysis, and electricity to develop machines, tools, and systems that perform efficiently and safely. Modern mechanical engineers also use computer-aided design (CAD), computer-aided manufacturing (CAM), and computer-aided engineering (CAE) to optimize mechanical systems.
